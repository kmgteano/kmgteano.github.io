    Welcome!

    I'm Katrina-Mae G. Teaño [teh-AH-nyo | tɛ.a'ɲo ]. Most people call me Katrina.

    Mathematician

    For as long as I can remember, mathematics has been my passion. Through Prep for Prep, a program for gifted students of color in the greater New York City area, I was graced with the unique privilege of attending The Brearley School, a fantastic private school for ambitious and inquisitive young women. While there, I took courses in calculus and statistics and learned to fold paper cranes, all of which influenced me deeply; I did a brief senior thesis on the presentation of crease patterns as diagrams to demonstrate origami structures.

    I then attended Florida Institute of Technology, one of the finest universities in the Southeast, and my love for mathematics only grew. I earned a Bachelor's of Science in Applied Mathematics cum laude from the university. I was the first student to receive the Lakshmikantham Mathematics Scholarship for excellence in mathematics, and was part of the inaugural Undergraduate Biomathematics Research Team, which did cutting-edge research using mathematical techniques to make sense of biology's most complex questions.

    I decided to stay at Florida Tech for graduate school, quckly picking up a Master's of Science in Operations Resarch, which tightened my focus on statistics, optimization, modeling, and discrete mathematics. My final program exam covered all of these topics; I also wrote a paper during my graph theory couse on crease patterns as planar graphs and the proof that the flat folding problem is NP-complete, which I like to think brought things full circle.

    Nowadays I am working as an educator, though my goal is to apply my background towards the rapidly growing and incredibly fascinating field of data science and machine learning.
